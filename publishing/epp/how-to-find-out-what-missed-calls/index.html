How to Find Out What Missed Calls Are Costing Your Business

May 14, 2026

Most small businesses know they miss calls. Fewer know what those missed calls are worth.

That doesn’t mean the business is doing anything wrong. Instead, it usually means the owner and staff are busy serving the customers already in front of them. The fix starts with measurement, not blame.

For two weeks, count missed calls during business hours and after hours separately. Use your phone system, call log, voicemail, or receptionist report. Don’t guess if the data is available.

Then classify the calls. New leads, existing customers, scheduling calls, urgent issues, sales questions, vendor calls, and spam should not all be treated the same.

New leads and scheduling calls usually matter most because they are closest to revenue.

From there, estimate conversion value. How many missed new-lead calls would likely have become customers? What is a typical customer worth? What is a booked appointment worth?

Even rough math is better than no math. It turns a vague phone problem into a business decision.

The handoff is often where the real issue appears. What happens when a call is missed? Is there voicemail? Who checks it? How fast does someone respond? Is the message complete? Does it land in a CRM or calendar?

The right fix might be call tracking, better voicemail, scripted intake, automated summaries, appointment booking, an answering service, or an AI receptionist. The call types should decide the solution.

None of this requires a huge transformation project. It does require a clear eye, a little time, and the discipline to fix one workflow before chasing the next tool.
